Design of A Commercial High-rise Building

Location: Tejgaon, Forum Dhaka

Project Brief: This project pertains to the design of a commercial high-rise building, which is a multi-story building that is tall enough to require mechanical vertical transportation, such as elevators. The site is located in Tejgaon, Forum Dhaka, where the road runs North-South and has a width of 80 feet. The concept behind this project is "High Hopes", which conveys a strong sense of optimism that something positive will occur or come to fruition.

Â© Farhana Haque | SEU

The design of this high-rise building is derived from the Dynamism Art Movement and is divided into two towers with separate entrances that are connected by a bridge. The podium of the building is made of steel or concrete and is characterized by horizontal divisions between the upper tower and the lower floors. It features banks and super shops on the ground floor vertical circulation by two capsule lifts. The third floor contains a large food court, while all other floors of the towers are commercial spaces. There are three bridge connections in total and the middle of the tower contains green spaces for visitors to enjoy the city views and cafeteria. The top of the building also contains green spaces that provide visitors with views of the surrounding area and ample airflow.

Overall, this project is expected to have a positive impact on various sectors, including the economy of the city and will give the city a new identity.
